## ðŸ“˜ 1. Concepts: Interactive Learning

### The Magic Link Protocol
Most online tutorials are passive. You read, then you have to Alt-Tab and try to do it yourself.
In Odie Academy, **Blue Links** are executable actions.

*   If a lesson says **[Add a Compressor]**, clicking it actually instantiates the plugin on your selected track.
*   If a lesson says **[Open Piano Roll]**, clicking it switches the view.

### Curriculum Generation
Odie can write new lessons on the fly.
If you ask *"Explain FM Synthesis"*, the AI generates a new **Lesson Card** with:
1.  **Concept**: The theory.
2.  **Visuals**: Diagrams (Mermaid/ASCII).
3.  **Actions**: Links to load an FM Synth plugin.

### 4. Vision & Infographics
Odie can also "draw" diagrams to explain concepts.
*   **Trigger**: *"Draw a diagram of a compressor's signal flow."*
*   **Result**: Odie uses visual generative AI to create a custom infographic on the spot.
*   **Use Case**: Visualizing signal routing, frequency spectrums, or studio cabling.

## ðŸ’ª 3. Task: Using The School

### Triggering a Lesson
You can access the school in two ways:

**Method A: Manual Access**
1.  Open the Sidebar.
2.  Click the **Graduation Cap ðŸŽ“** icon in the header.
3.  Browse the "Zones" and click a lesson tile.

**Method B: Contextual Trigger**
1.  In Chat, ask: *"Teach me about sidechaining."*
2.  Odie will reply with a **Lesson Card**.
3.  Click "Start Lesson" to open the full view.

### The "Check My Work" Feature
Some lessons allow you to verify your understanding.
1.  Complete the tasks (e.g., "Set the tracks to -12dB").
2.  Scroll to the bottom of the lesson.
3.  Click **[Verify Progress]**.
4.  Odie will check the actual state of your project and give you a Pass/Fail grade.
